IC Progress | Construction Photography

IC Progress provides the best construction site documentation services including photographic, video and time lapse documentation.

Interactive Floor Plans and Documentation

Our online software platform gives you the tools you need to get the job done in a timely, affordable fashion. Our simple three-step process, “Pin it, Click it, Ship it,” will increase your efficiency and workflow. Just upload your files (floor plans, photos, documentation) to our system and begin pinning photos onto corresponding drawings. Next, simply clip any relevant documentation to that photo. Once you’re done, you can ship out an email, completely organized, with everything your contractors need to know.This is the only fully digital service available with everything you need in one place. No more switching between platforms or dealing with digital versus analog files. We made everything work together so you can get your work done faster.

Secure Online Hosting

Our website is capable of hosting your images online so you can securely log in and view your images. Now, you can even order reprints directly from the site. No need to pick up the phone and call. You simply select the images you need, then we will print, label and ship the photos straight to your door!

Pre-Construction Photographs and Video

These photographs are the most significant part of construction documentation and act as your visual insurance policy. You should not be held responsible for damage to neighboring properties that existed prior to construction. Without photos or video of existing conditions, you may be held accountable. Have I.C. Progress document your site before you get started with any work.

Regularly Scheduled Progress Photography

Currently, many jobs have contract specs calling for regularly scheduled, independent, third-party photographs. Fortunately, the photographers at I.C. Progress are highly skilled and OSHA certified. Have one of our photographers visit your site to fulfill your contractual obligations to properly document and record your site through the demolition or build process.

Milestone Photographs

Every project has major milestones. Often times it is beneficial to photograph these moments. For example, I.C. Progress can document your MEP systems before insulation, sheetrock and ceilings are installed. Having these photos can save you time and money if you ever need to re-open the walls. It eliminates the guesswork of where problems may be hidden and can illustrate where the drawings fail to match with actual installation. You may also be interested in photographing pre-slab pours to document where various utilities lie within the slab. You can feel safe that our over-lapping photographic coverage will give you a good indication of where important utilities are if you ever need to chop into the slab.

Finished Photographs

After project completion, it is a good idea to have I.C. Progress photograph the site to record visual proof of the building conditions as the project is turned over to the client. For instance, if your client has any doubts that you may be responsible for incomplete tasks or damages, you will have verifiable proof to the contrary.

Marketing Photographs

You should be proud of the work you've done. Since you put in every last effort to make your project look outstanding, why not hire an I.C. Progress professional to come take high quality pictures of your site so you can show off your creation?

Video documentation

The I.C. Progress photographers are equipped with HDSLR cameras capable of shooting still photographs and high definition video. This is perfect for the client who needs both.

Time Lapse Photography

See your project come to life. Time lapse and now “hyper lapse” photography offer a great way to speed up gradual changes. I.C. Progress will help you select a vantage point for your project that will clearly show action as the job progresses.

Aerial Photography

An aerial perspective is instrumental in really showing detail that cannot be seen from ground level. Taking pictures across the deck of a new building does not demonstrate things like where utilities. Only when you get the camera high enough overhead can you really see some of these important details. Also, coming soon Drone Photography!

DIY Software

Don't have the budget for photography and need to take the photos yourself? No problem, the I.C. Progress online software platform allows contractors to sign in and add their own photos straight from a smartphone or tablet while still in the field. Just sign in, snap a photo and pin it to the floor plan. It's that easy!
